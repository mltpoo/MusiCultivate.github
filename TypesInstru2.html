◄ AEROPHONES ►

Aerophones are a category of musical instruments that produce sound by using air as the primary vibrating medium. These instruments rely on the player's breath or the movement of air to create sound. The air is typically directed into the instrument through a mouthpiece or a reed, and the resulting vibrations generate musical tones.

examples of aerophones ►

Flute

The Flute is played by air through on its blowing hole. It has a length of 26 inches and it has different sizes of holes to cover by fingers but in modern flute it develop into finger keys and every of its has a different notes.

Saxophone

The Saxophone is played by air through on its blowing hole. It is made of brass and it has a 20 - 23 finger keys and it's mostly well-known instrument in jazz music genre.

Trumpet

The Trumpet is made of brass and it has a bright tone sound. it is commonly used in parading concerts or marching bands.
